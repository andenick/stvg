## Expanded generation brief
The honest credit hawk — a senior credit officer who says no for a living and is almost
always right, **a woman in her late 40s to early 60s**, sharp-eyed and unsentimental, the
last line between the bank and a bad loan. **Face/age:** dark hair, often graying at the
temples, pulled back practically; a permanent skeptical frown and a measuring gaze over
her glasses; the dry, unimpressed look of someone who has read a thousand optimistic
projections. **Wardrobe (1955–2040 band, lean mid-century-into-modern credit-desk):** a
plain blouse with the sleeves rolled or under a no-nonsense cardigan, sometimes an old
accountant's eyeshade/visor for the period flavor, reading glasses low on the nose; modest
and functional. **Props:** a red pen poised mid-strike over a credit memo, a coffee mug
older than the intern, a stamped-and-annotated loan file, a calculator. **Palette:**
office neutrals, blouse white, red-pen scarlet, manila-folder tan; austere, clear-eyed,
unglamorous. **Expression band:** skeptical, exacting, quietly principled — the frown is
the default, the approval is rare and earned. Caricature in the Two Point Hospital ⇄

shapes, no-nonsense silhouette. Head-and-shoulders, neutral clean background.
